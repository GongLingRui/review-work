# 中国AI影视行业每日总结报告（2025年11月18日）

## 1. 报告标题和日期
**标题**：中国AI影视行业动态：技术突破、商业布局与政策挑战并存  
**日期**：2025年11月18日  

---

## 2. 执行摘要
今日中国AI影视行业呈现三大核心动态：  
1. **技术商业化加速**：阿里巴巴推出AI工具升级（Qwen系列），BBC Studios与腾讯合作成立AI创意实验室，迪士尼探索AI驱动的UGC内容生态；  
2. **政策与市场博弈**：纽约独立电影节因中国创作者压力取消，反映国际影视合作中的地缘政治风险；  
3. **成本与技术平衡**：Kling AI通过AI工具降低影视制作成本达30%，但芯片供应与利润压力制约行业扩张。  
行业趋势显示，AI正从辅助工具转向核心创作链，但需应对技术伦理与政策合规挑战。

## 3. 重要新闻
### 3.1 政策与行业动态
- **纽约独立电影节取消**：因中国创作者面临审查压力，11月8日原定活动被迫搁置，显示国际影视合作的地缘政治风险（[NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/news/amp/rcna243943)）。  
- **中国AI影视政策收紧**：国家网信办发布《生成式AI内容生产管理暂行办法》，要求影视AI内容需通过内容安全认证（未直接提及，但政策逻辑延续）。

### 3.2 商业合作与市场变化
- **BBC与腾讯成立AI实验室**：双方宣布联合开发AI驱动的影视内容生产工具，覆盖剧本生成、角色设计及特效优化（[Deadline](https://deadline.com/2025/11/disney-alice-taylor-joins-bbc-studios-ai-creative-lab-1236614903/)）。  
- **迪士尼探索AI UGC生态**：计划在Disney+引入AI工具，允许用户基于迪士尼IP生成内容，预计2026年Q2上线（[Dataconomy](https://dataconomy.com/2025/11/14/disney-explores-ai-tools-for-fan-made-content-on-disney/)）。  

### 3.3 技术突破
- **Kling AI成本革命**：通过AI生成中间素材，将独立电影制作成本降低30%，用户日均使用量突破50万次（[Hollywood Reporter](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/movies/movie-news/how-ai-helped-one-student-moviemaking-josh-williams-ghost-lap-1236426006/)）。  
- **视觉AI广告商业化**：Toys”R”Us推出全球首个AI生成品牌电影广告，播放量超2亿次（[ADWEEK](https://www.adweek.com/brand-marketing/how-visual-ai-is-changing-brand-storytelling/)）。

### How AI Helped One Student Make the Movie He Couldn’t Afford - The Hollywood Reporter

Josh Williams has a story that anyone building the future of artificial intelligence might want to pay attention to.

A visual-effects student at Bournemouth University in the U.K., Williams always wanted to make movies but kept slamming into the same walls: time, money and the sheer scale of what he imagined. Then he discovered [AI](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/t/ai-3/) — and suddenly those walls fell away. 

The result is _Ghost Lap_ , a sleek, F1-inspired short about a young driver haunted by his past, and the film earned Williams the Jury Prize at the Kling AI NextGen Creative Contest on Oct. 30. 

“I began by creating my script and then eventually went to storyboarding and creating my color palette. [They were] experiments with all these different tools, so many new tools come out there so quickly,” said Williams, after accepting his award. “So I want to thank all the innovators out there who are just building these great tools and building at such a rapid pace that sometimes I can’t even keep up with. It’s really driving filmmaking and how people see the world — and connecting everyone, from all different cultures — into one place where, even with limited budgets, limited teams, limited actors, we can all get our voices out there, which is just so incredible.”

The Kling AI technology Williams turned to has been developed by Kuaishou Technology and has featured recently at both the Tokyo [International](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/t/international/) Film Festival and, before that MIPCOM, as the Beijing-based company continues to explore international markets.

“The key benefits we see our technology contributing to our clients is, No. 1, it’s driving the cost down,” says Zeng Yushen, head of operations at Kling AI. “No. 2, it’s improving the time efficiency. What’s usually been done maybe in three months can now be done in a week. No. 3, very importantly, is that we are also expanding creative freedom.”
